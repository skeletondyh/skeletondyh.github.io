I’m a third-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at ETH Zurich. I’m fortunate to be advised by Prof. Thomas Hofmann. I’m currently interested in: (1) Understanding the training dynamics of geometric deep learning models; (2) Generative modeling for structured data in biochemistry (e.g., molecules, proteins).

Previously, I obtained my MPhil from HKUST, advised by Prof. Tong Zhang and Prof. Quanming Yao. Before that, I obtained my bachelor’s degree in computer science from Tsinghua University, where I worked with Prof. Jie Tang.

Selected Publications

  1. ICML
    Scalable Non-Equivariant 3D Molecule Generation via Rotational Alignment
    Yuhui Ding, and Thomas Hofmann
    In the 42nd International Conference on Machine Learning , 2025
    Summary PDF Code

    In this work, we relax the equivariance constraints in existing 3D molecule diffusion models. We use an autoencoder to learn rotations that align the 3D orientations of molecules. Then a non-equivariant diffusion model (e.g., parametrized by a standard transformer) is trained in the aligned latent space. Our non-equivariant diffusion model offers significantly improved scalability and efficiency compared with equivariant models, without sacrificing sample quality.

  2. ICML
    Recurrent Distance Filtering for Graph Representation Learning
    Yuhui Ding, Antonio Orvieto, Bobby He, and Thomas Hofmann
    In the 41st International Conference on Machine Learning , 2024
    Summary PDF Code

    We propose a novel architecture that combines state-space models with GNNs to improve the ability to model long-range dependencies on graphs. Our architecture achieves performance comparable to or better than SOTA graph transformers, even without positional encoding, and significantly reduces the computational cost.

  3. KDD
    DiffMG: Differentiable Meta Graph Search for Heterogeneous Graph Neural Networks
    Yuhui Ding, Quanming Yao, Huan Zhao, and Tong Zhang
    In the 27th ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery & Data Mining , 2021
    Summary PDF Code

    We propose a neural architecture search method for GNNs on heterogeneous information networks, which can learn task-dependent message-passing patterns that contain semantic information. Our search algorithm is differentiable, making the search cost comparable to training the derived final model once. Our method has good interpretability and is more efficient than attention-based models.
